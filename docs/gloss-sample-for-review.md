# Gloss rewrite — sample for review (item 2, approach B)

**Purpose:** calibrate gloss style + review cadence before the full 2,938-hanzi pass. 94 entries drafted, stratified across all buckets.

**Signal:** **28 of 94 (29%) had a factually WRONG source gloss** (not just malformed) — e.g. 现代文学 read as "Hyundai", 手机号码 as "...+ weight", people's names (戴望舒, 梁羽生) glossed character-by-character. 5 are AMBIGUOUS and want your eye (names vs. literal, date vs. digits).

Style rules I applied: **one concise learner sense**, drop aspect/measure-word meta unless it *is* the meaning, keep it plain-English and short. Please react to the style; flag any you'd word differently.

Legend: ⚠ = source gloss was factually wrong · ❓ = ambiguous, needs your call
